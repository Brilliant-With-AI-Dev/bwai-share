Part 1 of 4The idea
Part 1 · The idea

Move the hard part of a strategic meeting before the meeting

Leadership teams often spend hours discovering what each person already knew privately: where they agree, where they disagree, and what decision they are actually trying to make.

Common planning meeting

Start cold in the room

  • People answer shallow written questions.
  • The first hours identify what matters.
  • Candor is filtered by group dynamics.
  • The team leaves with less decision time.
→
Proposed Mirror experience

Arrive with the real issues surfaced

  • Each leader has a candid adaptive conversation.
  • AI synthesizes alignment, friction, and blind spots.
  • The team receives a concrete first draft.
  • The meeting begins with reaction and decision.

The product hypothesis

AI Mirror can turn candid individual context into a useful starting point for a specific leadership-team outcome—such as an AI Operating Model 1.0.

SPONSOR FRAMES THE DECISION
          ↓
LEADERS TALK CANDIDLY TO AI
          ↓
AI SURFACES ALIGNMENT + FRICTION + BLIND SPOTS
          ↓
TEAM RECEIVES A DRAFT OUTCOME
          ↓
PEOPLE DECIDE + ASSIGN ACTION

Why “specific outcome” matters: customers buy an AI operating model, annual plan, or investment decision. “Better alignment” is the mechanism, not the product.

Part 2 · Evidence

GoTu proves the combined experience was valuable—not why

The engagement gives us a strong reason to keep learning. It does not yet define a software product.

Observed

What happened

  • Leaders completed individual Mirror conversations.
  • The team left with AI Operating Model 1.0, rules, work streams, and ownership.
  • Ed reported clear value and roughly six recovered planning hours.
  • The “1.0” framing helped the team move.
Plausible

What may be reusable

  • Decision-specific interview schemas.
  • Anonymous synthesis across leaders.
  • Concrete outcome drafts.
  • Modules beyond AI operating models.
Unknown

What we still need to learn

  • What the artifact can do before facilitation.
  • Whether it is decision-ready or only a conversation starter.
  • Which steps need Nish or another skilled human.
  • Whether this repeats beyond one team.

The key limitation

GoTu experienced one bundle: conversations, synthesis, operating-model framing, Nish's judgment, group interaction, and follow-through. We cannot infer the contribution of each part from the success of the bundle.

What Ed's commercial interest means

Interest in other strategic uses, recurring EOS preparation, and a $5,000–$10,000 range is a useful signal. It is not a purchase commitment or proof of repeatable demand.

Where Voice Collab fits

Adjacent prototype evidence for async voice input, linked perspectives, synthesis, and agent handoff. Not evidence that the products should converge.

Part 3 · Learning step

Run a no-build reconstruction using work we already have

Recreate what the platform could have produced before the GoTu workshop, freeze it, then compare it with what the team created in the room.

Inputs

Keep before and after separate

  • Pre-workshop: Mirror transcripts, reports, sponsor context, company materials.
  • Post-workshop: final model, decisions, owners, work streams, notes.

Document any hindsight leakage; this is directional, not a blind causal study.

Owners

Three clear responsibilities

AdrianAssemble and timestamp the materials.
MattProduce and freeze the pre-workshop artifact; create the comparison.
NishAssess business fidelity and choose the next test.

What happens after approval

  1. Define the comparison first.
    Coverage, fidelity, specificity, friction, decision readiness, facilitation delta, and human effort.
  2. Generate the counterfactual artifact.
    Use only the pre-workshop evidence; freeze the result.
  3. Map the differences.
    Label missing inputs, synthesis gaps, schema gaps, human judgment, group interaction, and execution effects.
  4. Choose one next test.
    The result determines what should happen next—not a preselected roadmap.

How the result changes the next move

Nearly decision-ready

Test a no-room module with a new leadership team.

Strong conversation starter

Test customer- or partner-led facilitation around the artifact.

Material gaps

Improve sponsor setup, schema, or participant conversations; rerun.

Depends on Nish's judgment

Treat the near-term offer as product-enabled expertise and test partner reproducibility.

Success criterion

Directional evidence about what the artifact can usefully do before the room—and a clear next test. No product build, sale, or causal attribution required.

Part 4 · Decision

Approve one experiment, with explicit limits

The decision is whether this idea deserves one disciplined learning step—not whether the final platform should exist.

Approval means

Proceed with

  • Leadership-team strategic decisions as the opportunity.
  • AI Operating Model 1.0 as the first wedge.
  • GoTu reconstruction with the assigned owners.
  • Anonymous synthesis as the privacy default.
Approval does not mean

Do not commit yet

  • No product build or roadmap.
  • No architecture or Voice Collab convergence.
  • No pricing or packaging decision.
  • No permanent answer on facilitation.

Record your reaction

Nothing is sent. Copy your response if you want to share it.

Recommendation

Approve the bounded experiment. The evidence is sufficient to justify learning, but insufficient to justify building.
