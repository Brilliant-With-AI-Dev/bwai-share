How should BWAI send email?

Two votes in. Both back the same direction: AI writes the email and the product sends it. Adrian added a structural refinement — split product email into instant snapshots and delayed, evaluated Mirror reports. Below: what we propose to build first, and the one question left.

Updated 2026-05-19 Owner: Matt Audience: BWAI team Status: confirm v1 scope

Where we are

🥇 Decision converging

AI writes the email. The product sends it.

BWAI is the sender, never an individual. Same machinery handles every email type we will ever send: Mirror outputs, sign-ins, system notifications, future products.

  • Nish🥇AI writes & sends. We should be leveraging AI; the prompt upgrader and other product experiences will need this infra at higher volume; human work doesn't create materially more leverage here.
  • Adrian🥇AI writes & sends, with structural refinement (below). All outbound from BWAI. Invest in email infra now — auth, sign-in confirmations, and system notifications are weeks away regardless.

Adrian's refinement — two tiers, not one

Adrian endorsed the direction but reshaped the proposal. Product email is not one thing. It is two operationally distinct surfaces with different trust requirements, different timing, and different safeguards.

Tier 1 — Snapshots

Instant. Body-only. Email-first.

The lightweight "3 things" reflection delivered shortly after a conversation ends. Content lives in the email body itself — no attachment, no link to a separate document. Value is speed, clarity, immediacy.

Email-first delivery in the short term. Reinforces deliverability, confirms the inbox pipeline, creates a re-engagement loop outside the product. In-app view comes later.

Ship in v1

Tier 2 — Full Mirror Report

Delayed. Queued. Evaluated.

Deeper, more emotionally loaded, more sensitive. Warrants a delivery delay (~24h) for two reasons: gives the user time to sit with the original conversation, and opens a safety/quality window without adding manual friction.

A secondary AI evaluator reviews queued reports with access to both the generated report and the source conversation. Auto-corrects where possible. Escalates flagged cases to a human. Architect toward team pause/withhold for org/multi-user contexts.

Defer to v2

Proposed v1 scope

Snapshot email. One trigger. One sender. ~2 days of work.

  • What The Mirror Snapshot — the "3 things" reflection the user gets right after the interview ends.
  • Sender hello@brilliantwith.ai. Always BWAI, never an individual.
  • Body AI writes it, referencing what the user actually said. Lives in the email body. No attached document.
  • Trigger Sent automatically when the Mirror conversation ends. No wait.
  • BCC A BWAI team inbox on every send. Covers both team-visibility and future Linear-as-tasks forwarding for zero extra build.
  • Delivery Resend on a dedicated sending subdomain with full deliverability setup (DKIM/SPF/DMARC).
  • Replaces The mailto button on the Insights screen today. The mailto goes away.

Effort: ~2 days end-to-end. Stands up the full sending pipe — domain, deliverability, sender identity, AI body generator, send trigger. Every later email type plugs in.

What v1 explicitly defers

  • Full Mirror Report delivery. Not real yet anyway. Design when we build it.
  • 24-hour delay window. Applies to Tier 2, not the snapshot.
  • AI evaluator + review queue. Needed for Tier 2 only.
  • Internal review dashboard. Same — Tier 2 concern.
  • Self-learning click-through loop. Instrument click data in v1; act on it later.
  • BCC → Linear auto-task. Pipe the BWAI inbox to Linear's email-to-issue endpoint when we want it.

One open question

How does v1 know which email address to send the snapshot to?

The Mirror intake form has no email field today, and the auth token we receive has no email in it. v1 needs one of two paths to get a recipient address.

  • (a) Lookup via the auth provider. Cleaner if every snapshot recipient is signed in. No new UI.
  • (b) Add an intake field. Needed if guest (non-signed-in) flows should also receive snapshots.

Both are small. The team's call is which scenario v1 needs to support.

Follow-up asks parked for after v1

  • BCC Linear on sends so each outbound email creates a follow-up task automatically. Nish
  • Self-learning loop — capture click-through and engagement data; feed back into the AI body generator over time. Nish
  • Two-tier delivery primitive — first-class distinction in the email layer between instant (snapshots, transactional) and queued + evaluated (Mirror reports). Adrian
  • Evaluator dashboard — internal surface for flagged Mirror reports during the queue window: confidence, safety flags, source/output diff, "needs human review" sort. Adrian
  • Multi-user pause/withhold controls — architect toward team-level pause/review/withhold for org and multi-user contexts even if not exposed in v1. Adrian

Next step

If the team confirms v1 scope above, we move infra/006 from needs-triage to ready-for-agent and ship the Mirror Snapshot in roughly two days.

The two-tier architecture is the destination. v1 builds the foundation for the heavier Tier 2 path — domain, deliverability, sender identity, AI body generation, send pipe — without committing to the queue, evaluator, or dashboard until Tier 2 is real. The cost of changing course later is small.

Background — how we got here

The original question

When Mirror produces a report, how does it reach the user's inbox? Today: a clunky button that opens the user's own email program with half the report pasted in. Not a real send. The team needed to decide the shape — not just pick a vendor.

What we'd be sending

The emails

Mirror Snapshot and Mirror Report. The consulting deliverables themselves, not "your report is ready" notifications.

How many today

Maybe 5–10 per week while Mirror is being demoed. Will grow with paid engagements.

How personal they need to be

Very. The body is the user's own insights. A generic template would undersell what we just produced.

Who they're from

Always from BWAI — never a personal address. Mirror is a product, not a founder side project.

Other emails coming soon

Sign-in confirmations, system alerts, stakeholder invites for the next product. We needed a plan that does not get reworked every time.

What's there today

Nothing. No email goes out automatically. The current button is cosmetic and the experience is bad.

The two options we weighed

🥇 Picked

AI writes it. The product sends it.

No human in the middle. Instant.

When the user's report finishes, the product writes a short, personal email and sends it straight to them. Same machinery handles sign-ins, alerts, and anything else. A teammate-review step can be added later if needed.

🥈 Considered

AI drafts it. A teammate sends it.

Every email gets a human glance first.

When the user's report finishes, the product writes a draft and drops it into a shared BWAI inbox. A teammate reads it and clicks send. Slower, friendlier, and we catch anything weird before it leaves. Doesn't help with sign-ins or alerts — those need something else.
