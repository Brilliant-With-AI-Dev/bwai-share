GoTu · Pre-Workshop Experience

The Mirror experience GoTu will go through, start to finish

A faithful screen-by-screen preview · June 2026

This is a complete walkthrough of the guided AI-readiness experience the GoTu cohort will go through before their workshop. Each person opens a link, has a short voice-or-typed conversation with an AI interviewer, and walks away with a personal written report. This document captures every screen of that journey, in order, with a plain-English note on what's happening and why it matters. It's a draft meant for a careful read-through — not a final sign-off — so a few pieces are still marked as placeholder. Those are called out honestly as you go, and gathered in one list near the end.

How to read this

Read it top to bottom like a storyboard. The experience is grouped into six short chapters — arriving, the interview, wrapping up, the payoff, the close, and what's deliberately held back. Anything that isn't finished yet is flagged inline in amber, so you always know whether you're looking at the real thing or a stand-in.

Behind the preview

How this preview was produced

A bit of honesty about what you're looking at, so the rest reads cleanly:

The pieces of this experience were built in separate streams of work. For this preview we assembled them into a single build and drove it from the very first screen to the very last, capturing each step.

It ran in “mock mode” — the real, production screens, but fed with sample answers instead of a live person, and with the AI's thinking stubbed out rather than actually running. That's deliberate: it lets us show the exact shape of the experience — every screen, every transition, every button — without waiting on real AI inference.

So this proves the journey: what a GoTu participant sees, in what order, and how it flows together. It does not exercise the quality of the AI's actual questions or the writing in a real report — those depend on live inference, which mock mode intentionally skips. Think of it as the stage and the script, fully built; the live performance is judged separately.

Chapter 1

Arrival & setup

The first thing a participant sees, and the two quick checks we ask them to clear before the conversation starts.

01

The welcome screen

The front door. A single, calm promise — “Your AI readiness, in sharp focus” — with an honest sense of the commitment up front: roughly 45 minutes, about 25 questions, voice-enabled. One green Begin Experience button, and a quiet reassurance that they can pause and come back. The goal here is to set expectations and lower the stakes before anyone starts talking.

02

Set up your microphone

Because the experience works best spoken aloud, we ask people to take a moment to get their mic working — the first of two short setup steps. They can run a quick mic test, or choose to skip it and type their answers instead. Nobody is forced to use voice; we just make sure the option works before the conversation, not in the middle of it.

03

Watch this before you begin

Placeholder asset

The second setup step: a short orientation video that frames what the experience is and how to get the most from it. People watch it, then confirm and continue.

What's real vs. placeholder here: the gate itself is real and working — the step exists, it's required, and the flow moves through it correctly. The video itself is a stand-in; the panel currently reads “Instructional video coming soon.” The actual orientation video still needs to be produced and dropped in. Everything around it is finished.

Chapter 2

The interview

A few basic details, then the heart of the experience: an AI-led conversation that adapts to what the person says.

04

A short intake form

Before the conversation, a brief form collects the basics: name, email, title, company, website. The progress marker (“Step 1 of 6”) tells people roughly how far in they are, and the line “there are no right answers” sets the tone for what's coming. The little Empty / Matt / Stacey toggle near the top is a developer convenience for filling the form during testing — not something a GoTu participant would ever see.

05

The form, filled in

The same form once it's complete — here populated with the sample participant we used to drive the preview. With the required fields filled, the Continue button turns green and active, and the person moves into the conversation itself.

06

The conversation begins

The interview is a chat. A progress bar runs along the top so people always know how far along they are, the AI interviewer (shown as “AIM”) opens the conversation, and a simple reply box sits at the bottom — type or speak. It looks and behaves like a familiar messaging thread, which is the point: almost no learning curve.

07

A real back-and-forth

A few turns in. The participant's answers are the dark bubbles, the interviewer's are the light ones. The interviewer listens and follows up — reflecting back what it heard, then asking a sharper question based on it, rather than reading from a fixed script. This adaptive quality is the core of the experience.

Remember this ran in mock mode with sample answers, so treat the specific wording as illustrative. The shape of the exchange is real; the conversational quality of a live run is tested separately.
08

Finishing the interview

When the interviewer has gathered enough, it signals that the conversation is complete and points the person toward the closing questions. The experience knows when it has what it needs and moves on cleanly, rather than letting the conversation drift.

Chapter 3

Wrapping up

One last reflective moment before the report is generated.

09

Closing questions

Before generating anything, the experience asks three short reflective questions — what stood out, what they hope to get from this, anything they didn't get to say. People can speak or type their answers, or skip. It's a deliberate pause that lets someone add their own framing before the machine has the last word, and it gives the report richer material to work from.

Chapter 4

The payoff: insights & report

The reward for the conversation — first a crisp snapshot, then a longer personal report they can read, reply to, and take with them.

10

The insights snapshot

The first payoff arrives quickly: a tight, scannable “Your Crucial Insights” snapshot that names a few things that genuinely stood out from the conversation. It can be copied, downloaded, or saved as a PDF right away, and a small follow-up panel lets the person ask about what they're seeing. This is the immediate “that was worth it” moment.

11

The full report, revealed a piece at a time

Then the longer, more personal report. Rather than dumping a wall of text, it's revealed one section at a time (here, “Section 1 of 11”). The person reads a piece, can react to it, then moves on. The pacing keeps a substantial document from feeling overwhelming and makes the whole thing feel like a conversation rather than a hand-off.

12

Reply to it, and take it with you

The report isn't a dead end. People can reply to it — push back, ask for a tweak, or just say how it lands — using the same speak-or-type box, with a clear sense of how many replies they have. And the whole thing can be copied, downloaded, or saved as a PDF, so it's theirs to keep and share.

Chapter 5

The close

The screen that ends the pre-workshop journey.

13

“See you at the workshop”

Copy pending sign-off

The closing screen. It confirms the report is saved and ready, then hands off to the live event: “We'll see you at the workshop, where we'll build on what surfaced here.” A “chat with your Mirror” box stays available so people can keep asking questions on later visits. This is where the pre-work cleanly ends and the in-person session is set up to begin.

The exact wording on this screen (and across the report) is still placeholder copy awaiting final sign-off. The structure and behavior are settled; the sentences may change.

Chapter 6

What's deliberately held back

One thing the GoTu participant intentionally does not get before the workshop — and a behind-the-scenes look at it, for transparency.

14

The report ends here — no action plan

This is the same closing view, shown again to make one point clearly: when you read the report all the way to its end, there is no “action plan” section. That's intentional. For GoTu, the deeper, do-this-next action plan is held back for the live workshop — the report gives people genuine insight, and the workshop is where that turns into a concrete plan. This screenshot is the evidence that the withholding actually works: the participant's report stops short of the action plan, exactly as designed.

15

The action-plan screen (behind the glass)

Not in the GoTu journey

For full transparency, this is what the action-plan surface looks like when it is shown — a short list of concrete next steps a person can check off, plus options to share a version with a manager or a peer.

Important: a GoTu participant never reaches this screen. In a real GoTu deployment this route is dark, and — as screen 14 shows — the report itself correctly stops before the action plan. This is shown only so the team can see the held-back piece. Treat it as a behind-the-scenes artifact, not part of the GoTu user's experience.
