GoTu · Pre-Workshop Experience

The Mirror experience GoTu will go through, start to finish

A screen-by-screen preview · June 2026

“Mirror” is our name for this guided AI-readiness experience. In the week before GoTu's live workshop, each member of GoTu's team goes through it on their own. One person opens a link, has a voice-or-typed conversation with an AI interviewer, and walks away with a personal written report. This is the whole journey, in order — every screen, with a plain-English note on what's happening and why it matters. Read it top to bottom like a storyboard. A short, honest note on what's finished versus still in draft sits near the end, in one place.

Chapter 1

Arrival & setup

The first thing a participant sees, and the two quick steps they clear before the conversation starts.

01

The welcome screen

The front door. A single, calm promise — “Your AI readiness, in sharp focus” — with the commitment stated up front: 45–60 minutes, about 25 questions, voice-enabled. One green Begin Experience button, and a quiet reassurance that they can pause and come back. The job of this screen is to set expectations and lower the stakes before anyone starts talking.

02

Set up your microphone

Because the experience works best spoken aloud, we ask people to get their mic working first — step one of two. They can run a quick mic test, or skip it and type their answers instead. Nobody is forced to use voice; we just make sure the option works before the conversation, not in the middle of it.

03

Watch this before you begin

Step two: a short orientation video that frames what the experience is and how to get the most from it. People watch it, then click “I've watched this — continue.” This mic-and-video pair is a deliberate gate — clearing both is what gets everyone to the conversation on the same footing.

Chapter 2

The interview

A few basic details, then the heart of the experience: a conversation with an AI interviewer.

04

The intake form

Before the conversation, a form collects the basics — name, email, title, company, website — plus a few framing details: your LinkedIn, who you report to, and whether you manage a team. Eight fields in all, each required. The progress marker (“Step 1 of 6”) tells people roughly how far in they are, the timing line reads 45–60 minutes to match the welcome screen, and “there are no right answers” sets the tone for what's coming. We carry one sample person through the rest of this walkthrough — Priya Raman, VP of Marketing at Northwind Retail Group — and the next screen shows the same form in her hands.

05

The form, filled in

The same form, complete — filled in as Priya Raman. With the required fields in, the Continue button turns green and active, and Priya moves into the conversation itself.

06

The conversation begins

The interview is a chat. A progress bar runs along the top so people always know how far along they are — it tracks phases of the conversation, not a question count, so it already reads 25% on the first question. The AI interviewer opens the conversation; it signs its messages “AIM” — short for AI Mirror. A simple reply box sits at the bottom — type or speak. It looks and behaves like a familiar messaging thread, which is the point: almost no learning curve.

07

The shape of the conversation

A few turns in. Priya's answers are the dark bubbles, the interviewer's are the light ones, and the progress bar keeps climbing. This preview shows only a few representative turns — the live interview runs the full ~25 questions, which is why the bar here jumps ahead quickly. These frames show the shape of the exchange — how turns alternate, how the thread reads, how progress is paced. The interviewer's intelligence — listening, reflecting back, asking a sharper follow-up — is what the live AI does in a real session, and it's judged on its own, separately from this preview.

08

Finishing the interview

When the interviewer has what it needs, it says so, and a Finish Interview button appears — the clean hand-off from the interview to the wrap-up.

Chapter 3

Wrapping up

One last reflective moment before the report is generated.

09

Closing questions

Before generating anything, the experience asks three short reflective questions — what stood out, what they hope to get from this, anything they didn't get to say. People can speak or type their answers, or skip. It's a deliberate pause that lets someone add their own framing before the machine has the last word, and it gives the report richer material to work from.

Chapter 4

The payoff: insights & report

The reward for the conversation — a crisp insights snapshot and a longer personal report they can read, reply to, and take with them. The two live on one scrolling page, so the snapshot stays in reach as you move through the report.

10

The insights snapshot

The first payoff arrives quickly: a tight, scannable “Your Crucial Insights” snapshot that names a few things that stood out from the conversation, with Copy / Download / PDF right at the top. Below it, on the same screen, sits a short follow-up panel where the person can ask about what they're seeing. This is the immediate “that was worth it” moment.

11

The report

Then the longer, more personal report. Rather than dumping a wall of text, it's revealed one section at a time (here, “Section 1 of 11”). The person reads a piece, can reply to it in the box at the bottom — “3 replies left,” voice or typed — then continues to the next. Every panel carries Copy / Download / PDF, so the whole report can be saved or shared. The pacing keeps a substantial document from feeling overwhelming, and the reply box makes it something you can talk back to and take with you — not a dead-end hand-off.

Chapter 5

The close

How the pre-workshop journey ends — and the one thing deliberately held back for the workshop.

12

Thanks — your Mirror is complete

The closing screen, headed “Thanks — your Mirror is complete.” It confirms the report is saved and ready, then hands off to the live event: “We'll see you at the workshop, where we'll build on what surfaced here.” A “chat with your Mirror” box stays available so people can keep asking questions on later visits. This is where the pre-work cleanly ends and the in-person session is set up to begin.

13

Detail: the report ends here, with no action-plan section

Same close, fuller view

For GoTu, the deeper, do-this-next action plan is held back for the live workshop. The report gives people genuine insight; the workshop is where that insight turns into a concrete plan. The action plan is absent here by design. (What that held-back action plan looks like, for the team's reference, is in the technical appendix below.)
