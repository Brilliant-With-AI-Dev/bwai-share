Mirror — the right landing, every time

Today, opening Mirror can stutter through a confusing detour before it sets you down. This change makes it one clean step: the moment you open Mirror, the app quietly works out who you are and where you left off, then sends you straight to the one place that's actually true for you.

Read before we build it Off by default for customers No customer impact yet Proposed design · 2026-06-09

01  The problem today

Right now, opening Mirror sometimes sends people on a needless detour. A brand-new visitor briefly flashes through a page named “report” before getting bounced to the welcome screen — even though they have no report yet, because they have never done anything. The web address visibly stutters through two or three stops in a fraction of a second, so the app looks broken at the worst possible moment: the very first thing a new person sees.

🔗 Opens Mirror → 📄 “report” (empty) → ↩︎ bounced → 👋 welcome screen

The person ends up in the right place, but only after a visible flicker through a page that misrepresents what it is.

02  The fix, in one idea

The instant you open Mirror, the app checks — quietly, behind the scenes, before showing you anything — who you are and where you are in your journey. Then it sends you straight to the one place that’s true for you. One clean step. No flicker, no detour, and no page pretending to be something it isn’t.

🔗 Opens Mirror → ⚡ One quiet check, behind the scenes → 🎯 The one right place

03  The four journeys

Four kinds of people open Mirror. Here is what each one experiences now — and what was clunky before.

👋

The first-time visitor

Brand new. Has never started a Mirror.

Before Flickered through an empty “report” page, then got bounced to the welcome screen.
Now Lands cleanly on the welcome screen, ready to begin.
→ They land on the welcome & start screen.
📄

The returning visitor who already finished

Came back after completing their Mirror.

Before Risked being wrongly asked to “pick up where you left off” for work they had already finished.
Now Goes straight to their saved report, with no false “resume” prompt.
→ They land on their saved report.
⏸️

The returning visitor who walked away mid-way

Started a Mirror but never finished it.

Before Could be dropped somewhere that didn’t reflect their half-finished work.
Now Greeted with a friendly “Pick up where you left off?” offer.

Say yes, and they drop back into the conversation right where they stopped. Say “not now,” and they go to their reports area — their unfinished work is kept safely, not deleted, so it’s still waiting next time.

→ They land back in their conversation, or in their reports area — their choice.
🪟

The visitor still mid-conversation in another tab

Has a live Mirror conversation open elsewhere, and opens Mirror again.

Before Could be interrupted by a “resume?” prompt for a conversation already on their screen.
Now Dropped straight back into the live conversation, with no needless interruption.
→ They land right back in their live conversation.

04  The safety net

The behind-the-scenes check is fast, but the app is built to stay graceful if it’s ever slow or hiccups. A person is never dumped somewhere confusing, and never wrongly told they have nothing. The worst case is a gentle “give it a moment, try again” — never a dead end, and never a lost report.

05  Safe to ship today

🟢 This change ships behind an off-switch the team controls, and it is off for real customers right now. The moment it goes live in the codebase, paying customers see exactly what they see today — with zero risk. The team turns it on only in internal previews, until the design on this page is approved.
