What this is

One consolidated read of four product meetings from June 4–16. It tracks what was decided, what reversed, what to build next, and what is still open — reconciled across all four, not four separate summaries.

Who it's for

The Mirror product team (Matt, Nish, Adrian) and anyone who needs the current state of the product without re-reading the transcripts.

What you get

A ranked product backlog, a decision log with how each decision moved, the reversals to watch, and a single timeline to the July 14 Gotu on-site. Commercial/sales material is captured but kept separate from the product work.

Where the product started (06-04)

  • A working signup plus a one-shot report.
  • Gate the action plan behind a mandatory human call.
  • Group offering was just a sales rumor.

Where it landed (06-16)

  • Two products: self-serve individual Mirror + facilitated group Mirror.
  • "Guideposts, not gates" — no forced delay, no in-app call CTA.
  • Group Mirror is a defined product with a live in-room AI session.

Why it matters

  • The moat is high-quality context capture, not the report.
  • The whole roadmap now bends toward the paid July 14 on-site.
  • Clear product backlog, free of sales noise.

TL;DR

  • Product through-line: Mirror became a two-product system (individual + facilitated group); the durable asset is willing, high-quality context capture, not the report.
  • Central reversal: gates → guideposts. Mandatory human call and forced post-report delay both dropped for self-serve.
  • Critical path to July 14 Gotu on-site: retune Mirror prompt (one question/topic), force mic-test + orientation video, then deploy off Matt's laptop.
  • Biggest new gap: leaders vs. ICs need different interview tracks — ICs disengage on the leader-tuned prompt.
  • Most blocking open questions: Action Plan = separate stream or section? in-room AI mode? IC deliverable definition? All gate the on-site.

1. Executive Summary

  • The product crystallized from "signup + a report" into a two-product system: a self-serve individual Mirror and a higher-ticket group/team Mirror (group synthesis + facilitated session). The group product was a GTM rumor on 06-04 and a defined product by 06-16.
  • The most durable through-line: the Mirror's real value is willing, high-quality context capture, not the report. Stated 06-04, locked as a co-primary definition 06-09 ("if we don't do that part right, none of the rest matters"), reframed 06-15 as the compounding moat.
  • The post-report flow reversed twice and landed on "guideposts, not gates." Gate behind a mandatory call (06-04) → fork: leave-now vs. guided reflection (06-09) → strong recommendations + options, no enforced delay, no in-experience call CTA (06-15).
  • Output decisively split into two artifacts — the reflective Mirror letter (now) and the Action Plan (later). Proposed 06-04, decided 06-09, escalating toward two separate streams by 06-15.
  • The Mirror letter must land like a "gut-punch." Fix evolved from copy polish (06-04) → de-corporatize + success criteria (06-09) → sectioned reveal + "combative-but-caring" posture (06-15).
  • Insights moved to interactive, refinable cards that don't commit to memory until the user confirms (06-15) — the first concrete build in the new direction.
  • Onboarding reliability runs throughout: mic test + orientation video must become forced steps. Raised 06-04; by 06-16 two of the three non-negotiable pre-Gotu fixes.
  • The Mirror prompt problem was re-diagnosed as tuning, not structure (06-15): over-asks follow-ups and rabbit-holes; fix = one question per topic.
  • Leaders vs. ICs emerged as the biggest new product gap (06-16): the prompt is leader-tuned and ICs disengage; two interview tracks + adaptive intake now required.
  • A live in-room AI facilitation component became the headline group-product idea (06-16): AI with anonymized-Mirror context, synthesizing live, human-filtered, producing an end-of-session artifact.
  • Privacy hardened from a pricing footnote into a product requirement: company-can't-see-your-chat (06-04) → for groups: no long-term retention, court-order-level access bar, personal-email firewall (06-15).
  • Platform capability expanded (06-15): new create-step/create-task APIs + an internal SDK make agentic flows buildable; a build-your-own-workflow prototype exists (local-only, fragile).

Later (06-15)

"Guideposts, not gates" — no enforced delay; strong recommendations + transactional options; gating reserved only for intentionally-constrained flows.
