Mirror · design-first proposal

A workspace that feels like a letter Moe wrote you — not a dashboard.

The current prototype validated the flow. It also showed us the experience is wrong: two dead columns, plain chat bubbles, and a report that re-writes itself every time you ask for one small change. This is a proposal for what should be built instead — shown as static screens, not code. Three moments first; the full journey once we agree on the feel.

Issue #706Conversation-led · document-as-artifact · Moe always presentNon-interactive mockups
Finding 01 The shell looks like scaffoldingTwo columns floating in dead space; generic grey bubbles; a report boxed off in a side rail.

Nothing connects the conversation to the document. It reads like two apps side-by-side, not one place.

The fix → One warm, considered surface. The layout grows with the work: a focused conversation that the finished letter joins, never an empty pane waiting.
Finding 02 Refine is a sledgehammerEvery change re-streams the entire report for ~12 seconds — even fixing one number.

There's no conversation, no confirmation, no targeted edit. The document rewrites itself on every message.

The fix → Moe talks the change through in chat, then the letter redraws once, on agreement — with a version bump, a highlighted diff, and undo.
Finding 03 Choices make you typeWhen Moe offers a specific next step, you have to compose the reply yourself.

The friction lands exactly where the moment should be effortless — a single yes.

The fix → A prefilled, locked-but-sendable composer. The suggested reply is already typed; you send it with one tap, or clear it.
Journey · First finishyou've just finished your Mirror interview

You land in a conversation, not a teaser.

Moe greets you and names the three sharpest things it heard — sharpest first, right in the chat, no document yet. You can push back or steer before a single word is written. When you're ready, one confident button writes the whole letter. Notice there is no empty report pane — the surface is just the conversation until you ask for the report.

A Letter to Priya Raman, Who Is Running an AI Mandate She Has Not Yet Decided to Lead
Version 1 · written today · edits update in place for 24h

This is not a report. This is a mirror. Some of what you see will be uncomfortable. Read it anyway.

The truth. You are the most AI-fluent person in your building and the least likely to admit that fluency is now your bottleneck — twenty-one people waiting for permission you have not given them, and you are calling that "bottom-up adoption."

Do this now. This week, name one person to own AI for the marketing org — not a side project, a line on their goals — and pick one workflow to run end-to-end in public, where the team can watch it beat the old way.

Before it's too late. Decide whether your job is to be the best marketer at Northwind, or the person who makes twelve marketers better. The efficiency target will make that choice for you if you don't.

The Mirror

You can describe your own AI usage to the decimal point and your team's not at all, and that gap is the entire story. You run a marketing org across brand, performance, lifecycle, and creative. You drove the agency-to-in-house arc twice. You draft, summarize, and iterate with AI every day — that part is not in question.

Journey · Refinethe hero fix — a change you asked to soften

Moe negotiates the change in chat — and only redraws once you agree.

You asked to soften the hardest line. Because it's accurate, Moe pushes back once — gently — and offers a better deal: keep your letter honest, and put the softer wording in a version made for sharing. The document hasn't changed. Your reply is already typed and locked; one tap accepts. This is the difference between a conversation that occasionally updates the document and a document that rewrites itself on every message.
