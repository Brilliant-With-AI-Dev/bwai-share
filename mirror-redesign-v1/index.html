Mirror · design-first proposal

A workspace that feels like a letter Moe wrote you — not a dashboard.

The current prototype validated the flow. It also showed us the experience is wrong: two dead columns, plain chat bubbles, and a report that re-writes itself every time you ask for one small change. This is a proposal for what should be built instead — the complete journey, shown as static screens, not code. Three acts: finishing your first Mirror, the thirty days that follow it (the v4 proposal, visualized), and returning a month later to a Moe who remembers. The chat now wears the product's own design system; the voice composer is rebuilt from the ground up.

Finding 01 The shell looks like scaffoldingTwo columns floating in dead space; generic grey bubbles; a report boxed off in a side rail.

Nothing connects the conversation to the document. It reads like two apps side-by-side, not one place.

The fix → One warm, considered surface — chat built from the product's design system (avatar, asymmetric bubbles, one accent). The layout grows with the work, never an empty pane waiting.
Finding 02 Refine is a sledgehammerEvery change re-streams the entire report for ~12 seconds — even fixing one number.

There's no conversation, no confirmation, no targeted edit. The document rewrites itself on every message.

The fix → Moe talks the change through in chat, then the letter redraws once, on agreement — only the changed section, with a version bump, a highlighted diff, and your earlier draft always one tap back.
Finding 03 The composer is clunkyTwo look-alike boxes that swap by a hidden toggle, a red mic fighting a blue send, and blind dictation.

Voice is the heart of Mirror, but the input fights you: you speak and watch a decorative waveform, never your actual words.

The fix → One calm pill. The mic leads, typing is a tap away in the same box, your words stream in live, and choice-moments arrive prefilled and ready to send.

The voice composerrebuilt — voice-first, one calm surface

One pill. Voice leads, typing is always there, dictation isn't blind.

The composer today is two look-alike boxes that swap by a hidden toggle, a red mic fighting a blue send, and a stray checkbox — and while you dictate you see only a decorative waveform, never your words. This redesign keeps voice at the center but makes it one surface: the mic leads, typing is one tap away in the same box, and your words stream in live as you speak so you can trust it heard you. One accent, no checkbox, no mode you can get lost in.
