Act 4 · The leadership profilemonths in — the thing that knows you, made inspectable

"What are you assuming about me?"

Months of working together accumulate into one quiet foundation — not a report, not a chat log, the thing every surface reads from and writes back to. So the product lets Priya open it and read it in plain language. Every line says where it came from: something she told it, something she confirmed, something it inferred from a Mirror. Where she has corrected it, her correction outranks the guess and is never quietly reintroduced. And the categories that could hurt her — pay, ambitions, her manager — are walled off: never shared, never used to route her anywhere.

Act 4 · Your spacemonths in — the line between what's yours and what an employer ever sees

This is yours. Almost none of it ever leaves.

Months of Mirrors, plans, and working sessions have piled up — and the single most important thing about that pile is who owns it. Your Mirrors, your profile, your plans, your sessions are personally yours, forever; no employer, no salesperson, no internal routing ever sees inside the personal zone. The only things that cross the line are artifacts you deliberately make to share — derived, boss-safe, continuous with the share loop from your very first Mirror. The line is the product's promise, and it is drawn on your side of it.
