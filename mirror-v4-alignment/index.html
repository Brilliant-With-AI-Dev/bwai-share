⚡ The one question that could be a fight

D1: what is the destination — an assistant that becomes the product (end-state doc), or a multi-surface "exoskeleton" of product experiences in which the assistant is one surface (v4)?

Everything else in this meeting is sequencing, scoping, or naming. If you only prep one section, prep D1.

Bottom line

You agree on much more than the Slack thread suggested — the agreement list below is long, and it includes the things Adrian praised in the gallery. The disagreements cluster around a few separations v4 makes deliberately: Mo separate from the refinement flows, Return to Mirror as a distinct ceremony, the named assistant introduced late, and BWAI-facing Internal Docs as primary outputs.

Your instinct from Slack — "it's more a timing thing" — is a good reconciling frame, but go in knowing Adrian may not accept it as-is: v4 reads its separations as standing architecture, not as the early-months view of the end-state doc's world. The way to make "timing" a real decision instead of a vibe is the "what cycle one must not foreclose" list under D1 — it turns the frame into something testable.

One housekeeping note: the end-state doc spells it "Moe," v4 spells it "Mo." Same working title — and the two spellings in two docs are, amusingly, Adrian's voice-transcription point proving itself. Glossary: ICP = ideal customer profile; GTM = go-to-market.

Where you already agree

Don't spend meeting time here.

  • Mirror = the baseline/context ceremony. Voice-first, resonance over diagnostics (it should feel true, not just be accurate), useful discomfort.
  • Refinement is chat-based and section-by-section. v4's "release the Mirror section by section conversationally" is the same flow the end-state doc describes — and it's already mocked in gallery Act 1 and the workspace prototype.
  • Versions: permanent, dated, latest-as-default, prior versions retained and accessible. v4 lists this as resolved; the end-state doc's data model matches; the gallery's library screen (plus the click-to-open screen Adrian asked for) already depicts it.
  • Re-baselining is readiness-triggered, not on a fixed calendar. Small nuance: v4 allows "enough time has passed" as one trigger; the end-state doc insists the refresh stays event-anchored and user-initiated. Close, worth one sentence to confirm.
  • The ongoing assistant runs on accumulated Mirror context. Overlapping job description — v4: decision support, accountability, pressure-testing, routing; the end-state doc adds the chief-of-staff range and frontier-model orchestration on top. (That gap is exactly what D1 is about, so don't claim "same.")
  • Self-serve, pause/resume anywhere, no required human gate. v4 carries Nish's correction; the end-state doc's "no re-onboarding ever" is the same instinct for returning users.

The three decisions

Decision 1 · the lead

Destination: assistant-as-product, or exoskeleton?

What each doc actually says

v4 says End-state doc says
The product is a BWAI exoskeleton — Mirror, refinements, Action Plan, check-ins, Mo, Team Experience, future tools, Return to Mirror. Mo is a separate, time-boxed assistant surface (a 30-day included window, with a paid-continuation hypothesis ~$150/mo for Nish). "From the user's perspective, Moe is the product." Mirror is the onboarding ritual that seeds it; the assistant is the primary navigation.
Mo is one surface among many; the card grid is the home. Binding spec for a flag-gated demo build, distilled from founder decisions settled 2026-06-04 — carries founder-decision weight, but describes a demo, not a shipped product.

These produce different products at month six even if months one and two look identical.

Your proposed position

The end-state doc's destination, v4's first cycle. The 30-day window and continuation pricing become the on-ramp to the relationship, not a different product.

Saying yes means

  • We are building toward one product whose center is the assistant; v4's first 30 days are the front door to it.
  • The homepage starts as v4's card grid and later inverts to assistant-primary navigation — a known rebuild, accepted now.
  • The $150/mo continuation is a doorway into the relationship, not a separate SKU with its own architecture.

Three guardrails to state out loud (these prevent the month-six fight)

  1. The 30-day window prices Mo's active guidance, never the persistence of the user's context. The profile and accumulated context persist permanently whether or not the user continues — what lapses is proactive assistance, not data or memory. If an engineer implements "30 days" as a data window, the compounding bet dies silently.
  2. "Your profile leaves with you." Personal ownership and portability (end-state doc) must hold even when paid continuation lapses. Ownership and the paywall need an explicit boundary.
  3. Honest open assumption: the compounding relationship only exists for users who convert past day 30. If conversion is low, the destination is unreachable for most users. That's a product-model risk, not just Nish's pricing footnote.

What cycle one must NOT foreclose (the test that makes "timing" real)

  • a persistent profile beyond 30 days (no expiry framing anywhere);
  • an information architecture that can later invert to assistant-primary;
  • work-centric data organization, not a chat-thread store;
  • the returning-user experience stays a first-class build priority (the end-state doc's principle #1), built in parallel — the first-cycle funnel seeds it, it doesn't precede it.
⚠ If Adrian pushes back ("the exoskeleton IS the destination, not a phase"): don't argue him out of it in the room. The fallback that still wins: agree on the foreclosure list above. If cycle one forecloses none of those four, both destinations stay reachable and the decision can be made later with real usage data.
Say it like this "I think your v4 is the right first cycle, and I think the destination is the assistant becoming the center. I might be wrong about the destination — so let's agree on what cycle one must not foreclose, and we both keep our bets alive."
Decision 2

Return to Mirror: separate ceremony, or woven in?

The real disagreement

v4: a distinct re-baselining ceremony — "not open chat, not a rerun button" — with readiness triggers. End-state doc: returning users land with the assistant; the refresh is a targeted what-changed flow inside the conversation. Adrian's worry (his words): merge them and "the ritual gets lost in the noise"; his named open question: "how Mo's daily work actually feeds the Return to Mirror moment."

On the gallery screen he flagged: Adrian's concern is that Act 2 blends Mo and Return to Mirror into one interface, and the ritual drowns. He's right that the screen blends them. The fix isn't necessarily to un-blend — it's to make the ceremony's threshold visible inside the relationship.

Your proposed position — both, by role

The moment is a ceremony (named, framed, entered deliberately, with a beginning and an end), and the relationship is how you arrive (the assistant notices readiness, recommends it, hands you in, receives you back).

Honesty notes (volunteer before Adrian raises them)

  • His noise worry isn't fully answered by this. The "it's time for your Mirror" signal has to live outside the assistant's chat stream (homepage readiness state, email, a distinct visual mode) or it does get lost. Concede that and design for it.
  • This hybrid isn't free. One surface holding two modes (open-ended and ceremonial) may cost more to build than two clean surfaces. Confirm it's cheaper before adopting — Adrian costed the two-surface option honestly; this option deserves the same.

Leave open, with an owner

The Mo→Return handoff (readiness detection, entering the ceremony, exiting back to the assistant) is genuinely unsolved. Don't let the meeting file it as settled — assign who designs it.

One compliance check before agreeing

v4's Return wording is "repeat Mirror onboarding with additional context," and v4 requires refinements before any new Action Plan. Confirm the Return ceremony reads as "checking what changed" (the end-state doc's framing — "the system already knows me"), not as repeating intake. Required-refinement gates on returning users is exactly the re-onboarding the end-state doc bans.

⚠ If Adrian pushes back on cost Agreeing "ceremony-moment, relationship-arrival" is not adding a surface — it's defining the threshold between two things v4 already has. And the handoff he called the open question is the same work under either answer; separating the surfaces doesn't remove it.
Say it like this "I think Return to Mirror is both — a real ceremony with a clear start and end, that you reach through the assistant, who notices it's time and hands you in. Your noise worry is right, so the 'it's time' signal lives outside the chat. And the handoff design is the real open item — who takes it?"
Decision 3

When is the named assistant introduced, and what is it called?

The two positions

End-state doc: the assistant introduces itself after the first report's value lands — at the emotional beat, in possession of everything the Mirror learned. v4: the refinement flows are deliberately not visibly Mo; the named assistant appears after the Action Plan, as the 30-day surface. You've already conceded the timing point in Slack ("weird to introduce it — unless we did so very deliberately. Time for that is not yet").

Your proposed position

Adopt v4's later, deliberate introduction for cycle one — with one caveat that protects the core mechanic: defer the name, not the sensation of being known. Even unnamed, the refinement and Action Plan flows must visibly carry the Mirror's context forward, so when the assistant finally introduces itself, it confirms a relationship the user already felt rather than starting a new one. If the first 30 days feel stateless, the introduction lands as "yet another chatbot."

On the name (the caveat)

Your position is on record — "Moe/Mo" is working-title-only, never market-facing (legal exposure on "Hey Moe!"), real name chosen when one clears legal and everyone likes it. Note that v4 deliberately lists the long-term public name as an open item, so treat it as a tracked open question with your position recorded — park the decision, don't dismiss the question. Adrian offered to suggest transcription-clean candidates; let him.

⚠ If Adrian pushes back (unlikely) This mostly adopts his position. The only friction is the felt-continuity caveat, and it's cheap — it constrains copy and context-display, not architecture.
Say it like this "Agreed — introduce it late and deliberately, and it's not named Moe in anything market-facing. One ask: even before it has a name, the flows should obviously remember the Mirror. The user should feel known before they're introduced."

What each doc gives the other

↙ Take from v4

  • Action Plan as a first-class, versioned artifact with required refinements — the action spine the end-state doc lacks. Sequencing note: its "permanently accessible, versioned" promise depends on the end-state doc's artifact-lifecycle machinery (statuses, save semantics) being built first. Dependency, not parallel work.
  • The 30-day accountability loop (2–3 check-in emails opening a plan-aware experience). The end-state doc's nudge rules are the right constraints: every nudge can answer "why am I seeing this," restraint by default, never generic reminders.
  • Call choice as signal. Not a UI detail: skipping the call is itself signal that teaches BWAI how valuable the human moment is and for whom — part of how self-serve still produces relationship and Team Experience opportunities. Keep the principle; framing strength is Nish's.
  • Entry modes and exact-resume (schedule-a-focused-block; always return to the precise next step).

↗ Offer from the end-state doc

  • Leadership Profile — not a trade, a prerequisite. v4 has no memory model; Mo can't ship even its 30-day form as anything but a stateless chatbot without one. The profile (confirmed vs. protected categories, "direct user correction outranks inference," plain-language inspectability — "What are you assuming about me?") is the cycle-one foundation and the answer to v4's open question "how does Mo use history."
  • The share loop — boss-safe / spouse / peer variants, derivation strips confessions, fears, and family references, never auto-shares. Already a settled founder decision (2026-06-04), already mocked in Act 1, already praised by Adrian. It should slot into v4 as settled scope, not a new proposal.
  • Artifact lifecycle rules — when chat becomes an artifact, save semantics, statuses. (The machinery the Action Plan needs; see above.)

Internal Docs / Assets — give this its own five minutes

v4 makes BWAI-facing readouts (ICP fit, operator snapshot, outreach recommendation, Team Experience signal, risk notes, call angle) primary outputs, not afterthoughts — they're the mechanism that makes no-human-gate self-serve scale, which is Nish's correction made real. Confirm they're first-class. Which assets ship first is Nish's call.

⚠ Governance boundary to set before they ship The end-state doc marks categories of the profile as protected (compensation, career ambitions, manager dynamics — never silently acted on). BWAI-facing docs must not mine protected context for sales routing. Define the redaction line now.

Prefilled answers to v4's five questions for you

v4 explicitly reserves these for you. These are recommended positions with their source labeled — yours to confirm or overturn, and worth saying that in the room so the open questions stay visibly yours.

  1. Mo: one chat or multiple workstreams? Recommended (from the end-state doc's "organize around work, not conversations"): neither raw option — multiple areas of active work the assistant organizes for you, because threads are temporary and work is durable; a pile of user-managed chats makes the user do the filing. This is a data-model fork, not UI labeling — a chat-thread store built in cycle one is a rebuild later.
  2. Strongest concrete Mo use cases? The end-state doc's workflow shapes, in order: red-team a proposal · research preparation (prepare the brief, hand off to a frontier tool, interpret the result) · decision-support memo · prep for a team conversation. All four fit the "unfreezing coach" arc.
  3. Accountability loop? Yes to 2–3 check-ins, under the nudge constraints above — plan-aware, explainable, restrained.
  4. How does Mo use Mirror/Plan/check-in history? Through the Leadership Profile — everything reads from and writes back to it under its governance rules, because memory needs one accountable home: the user can inspect it, correct it, and corrections stick. Retrieval never silently changes what the system believes.
  5. Where do future tools appear? Contextually, via the assistant and homepage, never dumped up front. v4 states this; the end-state doc is compatible (ask-first navigation, work-tied nudges) though it never addresses a tools catalog directly — so confirm it rather than calling it pre-agreed.
