The shared frame (from the office-hours interview, 2026-07-07): the user has already paid — this is activation, not sales. The ICP is a senior knowledge worker in an AI fear cycle, drowning in "Very Great Ideas" content. Product thesis: AI skill is a frequency game — the pivot's job is to produce the first real rep. Chosen posture: first rep on their real work (not letter introspection), offered immediately, with genuine user choice and an XO-authored email rail as the fallback. 1
The invitation stays quiet

One card below the saved-letter confirmation, in the letter's own visual register. No price, no urgency, no button styling that competes with the letter. It reads as information, not a sell, which is the only tone that survives this placement.

2
The manifest is the centerpiece

Not rendered: the carryover

Nothing in the UI announces it, but the letter, action plan, interview themes, and named pain points were already loaded as XO’s working context before the first message. That is what makes the opening question specific instead of generic.

Annotations

1

The letter-grounded hook

The opening message quotes a specific, named pain from the user’s own Mirror letter (the Thursday review) and asks about this week, not about goals in general. Specificity is the entire hook: a generic “how can I help” here would read as a chatbot and get closed. Requires a generation step that extracts one concrete, current-tense pain point from the letter.

2

The rep happens before consent Trust implication

Real work is delivered before the user has agreed to anything or knows who they are talking to. Honest framing: the composer on this page is already grounded on the letter today, so the data access is not new, but the proactive first move and the withheld identity are. To a trust-sensitive ICP fresh off a vulnerable letter, discovering afterward that an unnamed agent was reading their Mirror can land as bait-and-switch. This is the variant’s core bet and its core exposure.

3

The post-hoc reveal and consent

Identity arrives only after value: “That, just now, is what I do.” The pitch is a receipt for work already done, not a promise. Consent is a real fork with equal-weight options, and the fine print states the decline consequence up front. The prep task finishes either way, so the help was never conditional on saying yes.

4

The decline path

Declining wipes the carried Mirror context (stated in plain words, and it must actually happen in the backend, not just in copy) and offers the existing email rail as the fallback: occasional Mirror-grounded check-in emails deep-linking back into the conversation. The letter itself is never touched. Declining costs the user nothing they already had.

5

What carried over invisibly

The hook in note 1 is only possible because the full Mirror corpus (letter, action plan, interview transcript themes) is preloaded as XO’s context. Fidelity of that carryover is a hard dependency: if the opening question misquotes the letter or picks a stale pain point, the whole variant collapses on the first line.

Annotations

1

Zero pressure on day 0

One quiet sentence under the saved card, one plain link, no button, no card treatment competing with the letter. The upsell-free constraint after the heavy letter is kept fully intact; the invitation is read, not clicked, by most users, and that's fine.

2

The email is XO's first authored artifact

XO writes the note itself: subject, body, timing. It references the Mirror privately and safely (never quoting content into email), and the footer says plainly that XO authored it and how to stop them. The deep link lands directly in the conversation, not on a marketing page.

3

Time-awareness on display

"Monday. One small thing." and "It's Tuesday morning. It's been six days since your Mirror." The greeting proves XO knows the calendar and the gap since the letter, which is the concierge's core credential: it meets a regulated, pragmatic person on a workday morning, not a raw one at midnight.

4

Consent before the first rep

Whichever touchpoint they engage, the consent beat fires once, right before XO uses Mirror context: plain ask, private scope stated, a real "not yet" path that still lets them talk. No pre-checked box, no consent buried at letter-end when they're raw.

5

The risk this variant accepts

Patience leaks. Many users will read the quiet line, skip the email, and never return; the first rep simply never fires for them. This variant bets that the users who do come back arrive regulated and convert deeply, and that trust preserved on day 0 is worth the raw volume lost.

Decision (owner, 2026-07-07) — A′ with D mixed in, plus a “generating context” transition

The recommendation (A′: in-thread introduction + read-only context peek + immediate first rep) was accepted, with two owner additions: D's patient rail rides along (quiet card, XO-authored check-in email with deep links, time-aware welcome-back greeting — the “not now” path, not a competing variant), and post-consent the surface moves through a “generating context” transition into XO's full-screen chat home. The transition is not re-reading — Mirror already read everything — it visibly transforms the letter, plan, interview, and profile into the rich context graph XO runs on, then lands in a full-screen chat whose time-aware greeting and bootstrapping chips prove the graph is real. See the “Chosen direction” wireframe at the top of this board.

Standing tradeoff (unchanged): it spends the letter surface's single-voice, upsell-free purity, betting a warm introduction with visible consent reads as continuation, not a pitch. Cheap to test, cheap to walk back.
