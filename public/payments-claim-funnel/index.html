Mirror — payment-first claim funnel: pick a direction

After someone pays for an Individual Mirror on Stripe, they land here: the "claim your Mirror" page that turns a paid receipt into a working account. Three on-brand treatments for that one screen, plus the full state set — the new buyer, the returning customer, the person who paid with their work email, and every error in between. Pick a treatment — that pick unblocks the build of issue #611.

00  How to read this

Where this sits: Stripe hosts the checkout (card entry, receipt). When payment succeeds, Stripe sends the buyer back to our page at /claim — that page is everything you see below. Its only job is to attach the Mirror they just paid for to a real account they can sign back into. Section 01 shows the three treatments of that page in its first-time state. Section 02 renders every other screen a buyer can hit on the lead treatment, so the set is complete before you choose. These are static HTML/CSS renders — faithful to the proposed brand, not the live React build.

🥇
Recommended lead: Treatment A (receipt card). A single calm card that does two things in order — confirms the payment landed ("Payment received — Mirror Pro"), then asks the buyer to claim. It is the direct continuation of the existing ConsumerAuthScreen, so it is the lowest-risk build, and a receipt-first card is the strongest answer to the one question every buyer has at this moment: "did my payment actually go through?" Treatment B (split celebration) is the pick if you want the post-purchase moment to feel premium and list what they unlocked; Treatment C (progress stepper) if reducing "what happens next?" anxiety with an explicit Paid → Claim → Start path matters more than calm.

01  Claim page — three treatments

These decisions are already locked (from #611). The treatments differ only in chrome and tone — every one obeys all of them.

  • The email is fixed — it cannot be retyped. Whatever email the buyer paid with is the email the Mirror is tied to. The magic-link path is correct by construction (we mail that exact address); the Google path must match the purchase email or we re-prompt. No silent binding to a different account, ever.
  • Returning customers upgrade in place. If the buyer already has a personal Mirror, claiming adds Pro to that existing account — we never mint a duplicate.
  • Paying with a work email is allowed and safe. If the email belongs to a company (B2B) account, the purchase still goes through and we create a separate personal Mirror for them. Their employer's workspace is never touched.
  • We resolve, then create — never blind-create. The server first looks for an existing personal account for that email and reuses it; only if none exists does it make a new one. The buyer never sees this — it is what keeps "upgrade in place" and "separate personal Mirror" both true.

03  What your pick decides

  • Which treatment? A (receipt card), B (split celebration), or C (progress stepper). All three carry identical actions and obey every locked decision — the choice is tone: calm confirmation, premium celebration, or explicit progress.
  • Which claim path leads? The mockups put Continue with Google first as the instant path, with the magic link below. Flag if you'd rather lead with the magic link (matches the plain sign-in page) — it's a one-line swap.
  • Show the price on the receipt strip? Mockups show "$240/year." We can pull the real amount from the Stripe session, show a generic "Mirror Pro," or drop the line entirely. Confirm the price and whether to display it.
  • Heading copy. Mockups use "Claim your Mirror" / "Welcome back" / "You're all set." Flag any you'd word differently.
  • Does success auto-forward? Mockups show an explicit "Open my Mirror" button. Alternative: forward automatically after a beat. The button is the safer default; say if you want auto-forward.
